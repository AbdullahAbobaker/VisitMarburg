Elisabeth von Thüringen

Also known as Elisabeth of Hungary (7 July 1207 – 17 November 1231), was a princess of the Kingdom of Hungary and the landgravine of Thuringia.

Early Life and Marriage

Elisabeth was born into the Hungarian royal family and was betrothed at a young age to Ludwig IV of Thuringia. She moved to Thuringia, where she became known for her piety and charitable works. According to a different tradition she was born in Pozsony, Hungary (present-day Bratislava, Slovakia), where she lived in the Castle of Posonium until the age of four. Elizabeth was brought to the court of the rulers of Thuringia in central Germany, to be betrothed to Louis IV, Landgrave of Thuringia (also known as Ludwig IV), a future union which would reinforce political alliances between the two families. She was raised by the Thuringian court and would have been familiar with the local language and culture.

Charitable Works

Elisabeth is famous for her dedication to the poor and sick. After the death of her husband, she used her dowry to build a hospital where she personally cared for the sick and poor.

Hospital in Marburg

Elisabeth founded a hospital in Marburg where she devoted herself to the care of the sick, following the death of her husband.

Miracle of Roses

The legend of the Miracle of Roses, where Elisabeth was caught carrying bread to the poor and it turned into roses.

Legacy

Elisabeth of Hungary is celebrated as a saint in the Roman Catholic Church for her charitable works and piety. Her legacy of compassion and service to the poor continues to inspire people around the world.

Elisabeth Church

Elisabeth Church in Marburg is a significant Gothic church located in Marburg, Germany. It was built between 1235 and 1283 and is one of the earliest purely Gothic structures in Germany. The church was constructed in honor of St. Elisabeth of Hungary, who was canonized in 1235, and whose relics were once housed there, making it an important pilgrimage site. The church's design includes notable features such as its twin towers, large stained glass windows, and intricate stone carvings. It also has a rich history tied to the Teutonic Order, which managed the church for many years. Today, the Elisabeth Church is not only a place of worship but also a historical landmark, attracting numerous visitors who are interested in its architecture and history.

Elisabethschule

The Elisabeth School is located in Ockershausen in the southern part of the city of Marburg. It is one of the three public high schools in Marburg. Named after Elisabeth of Thuringia, the school is a five-track high school with around 1100 students.
