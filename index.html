Famous Characters in Marburg

Discover the notable personalities from Marburg

Brothers Grimm

The Brothers Grimm, Jacob and Wilhelm, were German scholars known for "Grimm's Fairy Tales," which includes "Cinderella" and "Snow White." Their work preserved traditional folktales and influenced modern storytelling.

Learn More

Emil von Behring

Emil von Behring was a pioneering German physiologist who won the first Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1901 for his work on serum therapy, particularly for diphtheria.

Learn More

Elisabeth von Thüringen

Elisabeth von Thüringen, also known as Saint Elizabeth of Hungary, was a princess who dedicated her life to charity in Marburg. She founded a hospital and is celebrated for her deep compassion and care for the poor and sick.

Mikhail Lomonosov

Mikhail Lomonosov was a Russian polymath and writer who made significant contributions to literature, education, and science. He studied at the University of Marburg.
