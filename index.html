Elisabethkirche

Elisabethkirche, a stunning Gothic church in Marburg, is dedicated to Saint Elizabeth of Hungary. Built between 1235 and 1283, this architectural masterpiece is one of the earliest purely Gothic churches in Germany. Admire its soaring spires, intricate stained glass windows, and the tomb of Saint Elizabeth, which makes it a pilgrimage site for many.

Spiegelslustturm

The Kaiser Wilhelm Tower (popularly known as the Spiegelslust Tower) is an observation tower on the Lahnberge, which are located near Marburg in the Marburg-Biedenkopf district of Hesse. It was built between 1887 and 1890, is 36 m high and has 167 steps inside.

Famous Characters in Marburg

Discover the notable personalities from Marburg

Brothers Grimm id

The Brothers Grimm, Jacob and Wilhelm, were German scholars known for "Grimm's Fairy Tales," which includes "Cinderella" and "Snow White." Their work preserved traditional folktales and storytelling.

Learn More

Emil von Behring

Emil von Behring was a pioneering German physiologist who won the first Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1901 for his work on serum therapy, particularly for diphtheria, saving many lives.

Learn More

Elisabeth von Thüringen

Elisabeth von Thüringen, known as Saint Elizabeth of Hungary, was a princess who dedicated her life to charity in Marburg. She founded a hospital and is celebrated for her compassion.

Learn More

Mikhail Lomonosov

Mikhail Lomonosov was a Russian polymath who made significant contributions to literature, education, and science. He studied at the University of Marburg and impacted many fields.

Learn More

Capital of The Blinds

After World War I, 3,000 soldiers came to the University Eye Clinic in Marburg. The Institute for the Blind Studies, or "Blista" for short, was also founded in Marburg. Today, the city is considered one of the most blind-friendly in Germany.
